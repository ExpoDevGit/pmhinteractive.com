Privacy Policy

How PMH Interactive collects, uses, and protects your information.

Last Updated: March 10, 2026

PMH Interactive ("we," "us," or "our"), operated by Pablo Heckman, provides this Privacy Policy to explain how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information when you use our website at pmhinteractive.com and our games and applications available on PlayStation 5, PlayStation VR2, Meta Quest, Steam (PC/Mac/Linux/Steam Deck), Xbox, Nintendo Switch, and any other platforms we support (collectively, the "Services"). By using any of our Services, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

If you do not agree with this policy, please discontinue use of our Services. If you have questions, contact us at pablo@pmhinteractive.com.

1. Information We Collect

Information You Provide Directly

When you contact us through our website contact form or by email, we may collect your name, email address, phone number (optional), Discord username (optional), and any content you include in your message.

Website Usage Data

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect information such as your browser type and version, pages you visit and time spent on them, the website that referred you to us, and your general geographic region (country or state). This data is used to understand how visitors use our site and to improve it.

Game Data — Collected by Platform Services

Our games are distributed through third-party platforms (PlayStation Network, Steam, Meta, Xbox, Nintendo eShop). These platforms collect data on our behalf and in accordance with their own privacy policies. Data collected may include:

  • Gameplay statistics such as session length, level progress, and achievements
  • Crash reports and error logs used to diagnose and fix technical issues
  • Platform account identifiers (e.g., PSN ID, Steam ID, Meta account) used to associate game saves and achievements with your account
  • Device and hardware information used for performance optimization
  • VR-specific usage data on Meta Quest and PSVR2, which may include motion and interaction data collected by the platform's SDK — this data is subject to Meta's and Sony's respective privacy policies

We do not independently collect or store gameplay data outside of what platform services provide to developers through their standard developer dashboards.

Haptic and Peripheral Data

Some of our games support third-party haptic devices (e.g., bHaptics). These devices and their associated SDKs may collect usage data. Please refer to the relevant manufacturer's privacy policy for details.

Save Data

Game save files are stored locally on your device or through platform cloud save services (PlayStation Plus cloud saves, Steam Cloud, Meta cloud backup). We do not independently access or store your save data.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to respond to your inquiries and support requests, improve the quality and performance of our games and website, understand how players engage with our games, diagnose and fix bugs and crashes, comply with legal obligations under applicable law, and fulfill requirements imposed by platform holders (PlayStation, Meta, Valve, Microsoft, Nintendo) as a condition of distribution.

We do not sell your personal information to any third party, ever.

3. Legal Bases for Processing (GDPR — EEA/UK Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we process your personal data on the following legal bases: your consent (e.g., submitting our contact form), our legitimate interests in operating and improving our Services, compliance with a legal obligation, and performance of a contract where applicable. You may withdraw consent at any time by contacting us.

4. Third-Party Services and Platform Policies

Our Services integrate with or are distributed through the following third parties, each governed by their own privacy policies:

  • Sony Interactive Entertainment (PlayStation Network / PSVR2) — PlayStation Privacy Policy
  • Valve Corporation (Steam / Steam Deck) — Steam Privacy Policy
  • Meta Platforms (Meta Quest) — Meta Privacy Policy
  • Microsoft (Xbox) — Microsoft Privacy Statement
  • Nintendo (Nintendo Switch) — Nintendo Privacy Policy
  • bHaptics — haptic feedback SDK used in select titles
  • Fourthwall — our merchandise store at obsessive-shadow-shop.fourthwall.com
  • Formspree — contact form processing on our website
  • GitHub Pages — website hosting
  • Google Fonts — web typography

We are not responsible for the data practices of these third parties. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before using their services.

5. Cookies

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to maintain site functionality and understand how visitors use our site. You can configure your browser to refuse cookies or alert you when cookies are being set. Disabling cookies may affect some functionality of the website. Our games do not use browser cookies.

6. Data Retention

We retain contact form submissions and email correspondence for up to 24 months, after which they are deleted unless we are required by law to retain them longer. You may request deletion of your data at any time by contacting us at the email address below. Platform-held game data (achievements, save data, crash logs) is subject to the retention policies of the respective platform holder.

7. Children's Privacy

Our website is not directed to children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 through our website. Our games carry age ratings assigned by ESRB, PEGI, and other regional rating bodies — please refer to each game's individual rating for age-appropriateness guidance. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately at pablo@pmhinteractive.com and we will take steps to delete that information.

8. Your Privacy Rights

All Users

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, request correction of inaccurate information, request deletion of your personal information (subject to any legal obligations to retain it), and opt out of any future marketing communications.

California Residents (CCPA / CPRA)

California residents have the right to know what categories of personal information we collect and how it is used, the right to delete personal information we have collected, the right to correct inaccurate personal information, the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information (we do not sell personal information), and the right to non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights. To submit a request, contact us at pablo@pmhinteractive.com. We will respond within 45 days.

EEA / UK Residents (GDPR / UK GDPR)

In addition to the rights above, EEA and UK residents have the right to object to or restrict our processing of your data, the right to data portability, and the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. To exercise your rights, contact us at the email address below.

9. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. However, no method of internet transmission or electronic storage is 100% secure. We encourage you to use strong, unique passwords for platform accounts and to enable two-factor authentication where available.

10. International Data Transfers

PMH Interactive is based in Tennessee, USA. If you are accessing our Services from outside the United States, your information may be transferred to and processed in the United States, where data protection laws may differ from those in your country. By using our Services, you consent to this transfer. Where required by law, we implement appropriate safeguards for international transfers.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, our Services, or applicable law. We will indicate the date of the most recent update at the top of this page. For material changes, we will make reasonable efforts to notify users. Continued use of our Services after an update constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.

12. Contact Us

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us:
